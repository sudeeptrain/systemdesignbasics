TLS — A Sealed Diplomatic Pouch

Before two embassies exchange secrets, they verify each other's credentials, then agree on a private code only they share. After that, every message goes in a sealed pouch — a courier can carry it, but can't read or alter it. A TLS handshake sets up exactly that.
