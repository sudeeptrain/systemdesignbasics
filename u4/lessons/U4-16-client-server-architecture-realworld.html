Client-Server — Diners and the Kitchen

In a restaurant, diners at their tables place orders; one shared kitchen cooks and sends food back. Diners handle the eating and choosing; the kitchen holds the ingredients and recipes. One kitchen serves every table, and the tables never cook for each other. That's client-server.
