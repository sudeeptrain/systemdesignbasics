TTL Strategies & Cache Coherence

A TTL (time to live) caps how long a cached entry is trusted. Short TTLs keep data fresh but raise backend load; long TTLs cut load but risk staleness. With many cache replicas, you also need coherence — a write must invalidate or update every copy, or different users see different values.
