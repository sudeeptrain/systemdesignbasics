Concurrency vs Parallelism — One Chef or Many

One chef cooking three dishes by rotating between them — stir this, flip that, check the oven — is concurrency: only one pair of hands, but everything progresses. Three chefs at three stoves cooking at the same instant is parallelism. One is clever scheduling; the other is more hands.
