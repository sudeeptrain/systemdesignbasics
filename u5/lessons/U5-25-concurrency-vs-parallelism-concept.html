Concurrency vs Parallelism

Concurrency is dealing with many tasks at once by rapidly switching between them on one core — they make progress interleaved. Parallelism is doing many tasks at the exact same instant on multiple cores. Concurrency is about structure; parallelism is about simultaneous execution.
