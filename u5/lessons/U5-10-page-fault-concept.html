Page Fault

When a program accesses a page that isn't in RAM, the CPU raises a page fault. The OS pauses the program, fetches the page from disk into a free frame, updates the page table, and resumes — so the access succeeds. It's transparent, but a disk fetch is thousands of times slower than RAM.
