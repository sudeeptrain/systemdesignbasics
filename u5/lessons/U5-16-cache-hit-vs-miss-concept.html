Cache Hit vs Cache Miss

A cache holds copies of recently-used data close at hand. If the data you want is there, it's a hit — fast. If not, it's a miss — you go to the slow backing store, then usually copy the result into the cache so next time hits. Hit rate is what makes or breaks performance.
