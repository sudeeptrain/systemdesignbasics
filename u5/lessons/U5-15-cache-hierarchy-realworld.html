Cache Hierarchy — From Countertop to Basement

When you cook, the thing in your hand is instant; the spice rack is a reach away; the pantry is a few steps; the basement store is a trek. You keep what you use most closest. A CPU stacks memory the same way — L1 in hand, RAM in the basement — and pulls what it fetches up close for next time.
