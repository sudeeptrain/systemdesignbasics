Preemptive vs Non-Preemptive Scheduling

Non-preemptive scheduling lets a running process keep the CPU until it finishes or blocks — simple, but one long job makes everyone wait. Preemptive scheduling can interrupt it after a time slice, so short jobs stay responsive. Responsiveness vs simplicity.
